## TDEE Estimation Accuracy — Verified March 2026

### Mifflin-St Jeor (best available equation):
- Predicts RMR within 10% in 70% of obese individuals (vs 87% in non-obese)
- Up to 21% underestimations in obese population
- Systematic review: PubMed 15883556 (Frankenfield et al., 2005)

### Activity factor additional error:
- Discrete "buckets" (1.375 vs 1.55) can add 4-6% error
- Real activity level may fall between buckets
- MacroFactor analysis confirms TDEE calculators are "routinely off by several hundred calories"

### Total accumulated error:
- TDEE estimate can deviate +/- 300-400 kcal from reality
- For someone with calculated TDEE of 2800-3100, real TDEE could be 2400-3500

### How to apply:
ALWAYS present TDEE calculations as STARTING POINTS with uncertainty ranges, not precise values. The only way to determine true TDEE is through 3-4 weeks of consistent tracking (daily weight + caloric intake) and adjusting based on actual weight trends.
