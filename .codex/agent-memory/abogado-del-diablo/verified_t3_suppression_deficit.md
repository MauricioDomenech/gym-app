## Mechanism — Verified (PMC 2649744)

- Caloric RESTRICTION (not negative energy balance per se) reduces circulating T3
- Mechanism: decreased T4→T3 conversion, increased rT3 (reverse T3) production
- This is an ADAPTIVE response to conserve energy and protein during energy stress
- Effects are comparable to mild hypothyroidism at tissue level
- Triggered by: caloric deficit, overreaching training, acute illness — or the combination

## When clinically relevant (risk accumulates with):

1. Deficit >25-30% of TDEE sustained >6-8 weeks
2. High training volume simultaneously (exacerbates the stress signal)
3. Low carbohydrate intake (T3 conversion requires adequate glucose/insulin)
4. Duration: cumulative effect — worsens over time without diet break

## Restoration:

- T3 normalizes during diet break at maintenance calories
- Requires minimum 7-10 days at true maintenance for meaningful restoration
- 1 week may be insufficient for COMPLETE normalization; 10-14 days is better
- The BREAK Study (PMC 10642783) is testing IER protocols for this; results pending

## MATADOR (PMC 5803575) caveats:

- Fat loss 12.3 kg (INT) vs 8.0 kg (CON) — figures verified
- Population: OBESE men WITHOUT resistance training of high volume
- Protocol: 2 weeks deficit + 2 weeks maintenance (NOT 4+1 as proposed)
- NOT directly extrapolable to resistance-trained athletes with 11 sessions/week
- The PRINCIPLE (periodic breaks reduce adaptive thermogenesis) is valid; the EXACT protocol and magnitude are not validated in trained populations

## Overtraining syndrome risk with 11 sessions/week + deficit (PMC 3435910):

- OTS results from combination: excessive training volume + inadequate calories/protein/carbs
- 11 sessions/week + 1824 kcal + low carbs for glycogen = classic OTS setup
- Markers: blunted cortisol response, elevated resting HR, reduced HRV, URI susceptibility
- At ~175g carbs/day for someone training 11 sessions/week, glycogen may be chronically underfilled
- Risk window: 4-6 weeks in this configuration without break
