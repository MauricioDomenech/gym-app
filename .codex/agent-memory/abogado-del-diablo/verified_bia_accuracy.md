## BIA (Bioimpedance) Accuracy vs DEXA — Verified March 2026

### Key findings:
- **Body Fat %**: Multi-frequency BIA (InBody 770, professional) has bias of -4.0 +/- 2.8% vs DEXA (Frontiers in Nutrition 2024)
- **Fat-Free Mass**: Overpredicted by 2.8 +/- 2.1 kg vs DEXA
- **Between-day variability**: 0.1-0.7 kg in body mass measurements
- **Single-frequency BIA** (home scales): mean difference -5.5 +/- 2.9% in body fat vs DEXA
- **Limits of agreement**: approximately -5% to +5% for multi-frequency; wider for single-frequency
- **Accuracy by BMI**: Lower in overweight/obese (70-75%) vs non-obese (87%) for Mifflin-St Jeor

### Practical implication:
A change of 0.19 kg in "muscle mass" on a home BIA scale is WITHIN biological noise (0.1-0.7 kg between-day variability). Cannot be interpreted as meaningful at individual level.

### How to apply:
ALWAYS flag when coaches use weekly BIA body composition changes (muscle mass, body fat %) as evidence of progress or regression. BIA is useful only for TRENDS over 8+ weeks with consistent measurement conditions.
