## Murphy 2022 (PMID 34623696) — What it actually says

- Meta-analysis, Scandinavian Journal of Medicine & Science in Sports
- Déficit ~500 kcal/día PREVENTS lean mass GAINS (ES = -0.57, p=0.02)
- Strength gains are NOT impaired (ES = -0.31, p=0.28) — critical distinction
- Quote: "Individuals performing RT to PRESERVE LM during weight loss should avoid energy deficits >500 kcal/day"
- The "700 kcal catabolic threshold" cited by some coaches does NOT appear explicitly in Murphy 2022
- Risk is a continuum: 500-700 kcal = impairs gains; >700 kcal = higher risk of actual LBM loss
- Population was heterogeneous (not exclusively lean athletes)

## Helms 2014 (PMID 24092765) — What it actually says

- Systematic review, IJSNEM
- Population: LEAN resistance-trained athletes (bodybuilders, strength athletes in prep)
- Recommendation: 2.3-3.1 g/kg FFM, scaled UPWARD with severity of deficit and leanness
- NOT directly applicable to overweight individuals (18% BF): for them, 1.5-2.0 g/kg total BW is more relevant
- For someone at 18% BF in 27% deficit: use minimum 2.3 g/kg FFM as conservative protective measure

## Practical application for user (Mauri, ~18% BF, LBM ~83 kg):

- At 745 kcal real deficit + 172g protein (1.69 g/kg): YELLOW zone
- Murphy: exceeds 500 kcal threshold, inhibits gains and risks LBM loss
- Helms: 172g < 191g minimum (2.3 × 83 kg FFM) for this deficit magnitude
- FIX: Raise protein to 195-200g/day → qualifies as protected deficit

## Key distinction: Garthe 2011 (PMID 21558571)

- Elite athletes, 0.7% vs 1.4% BW loss/week
- 0.7% preserved LBM better and allowed strength GAINS
- Study population: lean elite athletes (~73 kg, low BF)
- Nuckols adaptation for higher BF: %BF/20 = % BW/week safe rate
- For 18% BF: 18/20 = 0.9%/week → user's 0.65-0.85 kg/week is WITHIN range

## Safe weight loss rate for Mauri (101.5 kg, 18% BF):

- Nuckols upper limit: 0.9%/week = ~0.91 kg/week
- Target: 0.5-0.7 kg/week (optimal)
- 0.85 kg/week: yellow but within Nuckols range — monitor weekly
