## Fat Intake and Hormonal Health in Men — Verified March 2026

### Key meta-analysis: Whittaker et al. 2021 (JSBMB)
- Low-fat diets (~20% calories) reduce testosterone 10-15% in men
- Effect size: -0.38 (95% CI -0.75 to -0.01, P = 0.04)
- European men: stronger effect -0.52
- Vegetarian low-fat diets: up to 26% testosterone reduction
- PubMed: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33741447/

### Updated: Soltani et al. 2025 meta-analysis
- Confirmed significant decreases in sex hormones on low-fat vs high-fat diets
- Published in Journal of Food Science
- https://ift.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/1750-3841.70266

### The "0.8 g/kg minimum" claim:
- NOT from a single specific study
- It's a practical guideline derived from:
  - 20% of maintenance calories threshold (from multiple studies)
  - ISSN recommendations of 20-35% of total calories
  - Practical translation to g/kg for convenience
- The REAL evidence-based threshold is: 20% of MAINTENANCE calories (not deficit calories)
- Important distinction: In deficit, the same g/kg represents a HIGHER percentage of total calories

### How to apply:
When evaluating fat intake for someone in deficit:
1. Check BOTH the absolute grams AND the percentage of total calories
2. Someone eating 67g fat on 2183 kcal = 27.6% — acceptable by percentage
3. BUT 67g at 103kg = 0.65 g/kg — may be low in absolute terms for a large man
4. For men over 40 in deficit: recommend 75-85g/dia (30-35% of deficit calories) as safety margin
5. Always cite the ACTUAL evidence (percentage-based) not the derived guideline (g/kg)
