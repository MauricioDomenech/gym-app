## Vitamin D Deficiency in Spain — Verified March 2026

### Prevalence:
- 88% of Spanish population has levels < 30 ng/mL (suboptimal)
- 37% has levels < 20 ng/mL (deficient)
- Over 65: 80-100% have levels < 20 ng/mL
- Paradoxical for a Mediterranean/sunny country

### Why the paradox:
- Low dietary provision
- Sun protection behaviors in summer
- Much of Spain is above 35°N parallel (low synthesis in winter/spring)
- Study: Nature Scientific Reports 2022 confirmed this paradox

### Supplementation evidence:
- 2000 IU/day preferred over 1000 IU/day (PubMed 38337676, 2024 review)
- 2000 IU maintains levels >30 ng/mL in >90% of adults
- 1000 IU insufficient for many people
- Safe for several years even if already sufficient at baseline
- Take WITH a fat-containing meal (vitamin D is fat-soluble)

### How to apply:
For Spanish users, vitamin D supplementation is NOT "generic advice" — it's highly relevant given 88% suboptimal prevalence. Recommend 2000 IU/day, ideally confirmed with blood test (25-OH vitamin D).
