## Cooking Conversion Factors (Cooked / Raw) — Verified March 2026

### Grains
| Food | Factor (cooked = raw x factor) | Source |
|------|-------------------------------|--------|
| White rice | x2.8 - x3.0 | Weigh School (75g raw → 214g cooked = 2.85x) |
| Brown rice | x2.0 - x2.5 | Weigh School (75g raw → 175g cooked = 2.33x); lower than white due to fiber |
| Whole wheat pasta | x2.4 - x2.8 | Omnicalculator; higher fiber = less water absorption than regular pasta |
| Regular pasta | x2.0 - x2.5 | Multiple sources; depends heavily on cooking time |

### Legumes
| Food | Factor (cooked = raw x factor) | Source |
|------|-------------------------------|--------|
| Lentils | x2.5 - x3.0 | Earth to Veg; 1 cup dry → ~2.5-3 cups cooked |
| Chickpeas | x2.5 - x3.0 | Earth to Veg; similar to lentils |

### Common errors found in coach recommendations:
- White rice factor /2.5 is TOO LOW (should be /2.8-3.0) → underestimates raw weight needed
- Brown rice factor /2.8 is TOO HIGH (should be /2.0-2.5) → overestimates raw weight needed
- These errors go in OPPOSITE directions, which can confuse macro tracking

### How to apply:
ALWAYS recommend users weigh foods RAW and use raw nutritional data. This eliminates all conversion factor uncertainty. When cooked weights must be used, use the middle of the range and accept ~10% margin of error.
